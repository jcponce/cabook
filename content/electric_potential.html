Electric potential

In physics we learn that if an electric potential $\phi$ is determined by static electric charges, $\phi$ must satisfy Laplace's equation (that is, be harmonic). The conjugate function $\Phi$ of $\phi$ is interpreted as follows: Lines aling which $\Phi$ is constant are lines aling which forces act on thest charges and are called flux lines. Tangent vectors to such lines are $\text{grad }\phi = \mathbf E$, called the electric field, see Figure. Thus the flux lines and the equipotential lines (lines of constant $\phi$) intersect orthogonally.
